# 🎯 CUSTOMER INTERVIEW GUIDE & FEEDBACK LOOP

## Why Customer Research Matters

**You have a hypothesis:**
"Hosts will pay $20 for gap analysis because it increases bookings by 23%"

**Before scaling to 10,000 emails, validate with 10 customers.**

This guide shows you how to:
1. Get honest feedback from early customers
2. Find what really resonates
3. Identify hidden objections
4. Iterate before scaling

---

## Interview Template (15-Minute Calls)

### Pre-Call (Send via email):

```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for trying [Product Name]! 

I'd love to get your feedback on the experience (15 min call).

Quick questions:
- Did the analysis make sense?
- What would make it better?
- Would you use this again?
- Who else should we talk to?

Calendar link: [Calendly]

Looking forward to chatting!
```

### During Call: Script

**Opening (1 min):**
```
"Thanks for hopping on! I'm really curious to hear about your experience with [Product]. 
No sales pitch, just honest feedback. Sound good?"
```

**Core Questions (10 min):**

1. **Discovery**
   "Walk me through your experience. What was your first impression?"
   *Listen for:* Initial reaction, friction points

2. **Value Clarity**
   "Before you used this, what was your biggest challenge with photos?"
   *Listen for:* Do they actually have the problem we think?

3. **Solution Fit**
   "Did the analysis help? What was most useful?"
   *Listen for:* Did our solution actually solve their problem?

4. **Price Sensitivity**
   "Would you pay $50 for this instead of $20?"
   *Listen for:* How much would they actually pay?

5. **Willingness to Pay More**
   "What if we did this monthly for $20/month - would that interest you?"
   *Listen for:* Do they want recurring analysis?

6. **Competitive Alternative**
   "How would you solve this without us?"
   *Listen for:* Do they have alternative solutions?

7. **Referrals**
   "Who else in your network should we talk to?"
   *Listen for:* Can they become advocates?

8. **Net Promoter Score**
   "On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend us?"
   *Listen for:* If <8, ask why

**Closing (2 min):**
```
"This is super helpful. Thanks for your time. 
Can I reach out in a month to see how it's working?"
```

## Post-Call: Analysis

### For Each Call, Answer:

1. **Problem/Solution Fit** (Doesn't have / Has it / Solved it)
   - Did they actually have this problem?
   - Did our solution fix it?

2. **Price Sensitivity** ($20 / $50 / $100+)
   - What would they actually pay?
   - Are we underpriced?

3. **Usage Pattern**
   - One-time purchase? Or recurring?
   - Do they need monthly analysis?

4. **Key Quote** (Capture verbatim)
   - "I didn't realize hot tub photos made that big a difference"
   - "I'd pay $50/month if you tracked my bookings over time"
   - "Everyone asks me about the pool, so I need those photos"

5. **Action** (What to do next)
   - Follow up with upsell?
   - Ask for testimonial?
   - Get referral intro?

## What You're Looking For (Red Flags vs. Green Flags)

### 🟢 GREEN FLAGS (Strong Product-Market Fit)

- "This is exactly what I needed"
- "I'll definitely recommend you"
- "Can I sign up for monthly reports?"
- "How do I upgrade to premium?"
- "My friends should use this"

### 🔴 RED FLAGS (Problem/Solution Mismatch)

- "It's nice, but I'm not sure I'll use it again"
- "The price seems high for what it is"
- "I could do this myself"
- "My guests don't seem to care about photos"
- "I tried it but my bookings didn't increase"

### 🟡 YELLOW FLAGS (Iterate & Test)

- "It's helpful but expensive"
- "I'd use this if it did X instead"
- "It's good but my competitors offer something similar"
- "I might upgrade in a few months"

## Iteration Framework

### If NPS Average is:

**8-10 (Excellent Product-Market Fit)**
- ✅ Scale aggressively
- ✅ Add premium tier
- ✅ Get testimonials & case studies
- ✅ Expand to adjacent products

**6-7 (Good but needs work)**
- 🟡 Interview deeper on objections
- 🟡 Test price reductions
- 🟡 Test feature additions
- 🟡 Focus on highest-NPS segment

**<6 (Weak Product-Market Fit)**
- ❌ Stop scaling
- ❌ Redesign core offering
- ❌ Pivot to different customer segment
- ❌ Question core assumption

## Micro Feedback Loop (Continuous)

**For every customer who pays, get feedback within 48 hours:**

**Email:**
```
Hi [Name],

Quick question: On a scale of 0-10, how valuable was [Product]?

Reply with just a number (0-10).

If <7: I'd love to understand why. Can we jump on a quick call?

Thanks!
```

**Track:**
- Daily average NPS
- Week-over-week trend
- Why scores dropped (if they do)

## A/B Testing Framework

**Once you have 5+ customers, A/B test:**

### Email Subject Lines (Test 2 at a time)

```
A: "You're leaving $7,800 on the table"
B: "The #1 reason you're not fully booked"
Send to 100 each, measure open rate
Winner gets used for remaining sends
```

### Landing Page Copy (Test 2 at a time)

```
A: "Find Your Photo Gaps" ($20)
B: "Get +23% More Bookings" ($50)
Send 50 each, measure conversion rate
Winner gets used for scaling
```

### Price Point (Test 3 at a time)

```
A: $20 (baseline)
B: $35
C: $50
Each to 50 people, measure:
  - Conversion rate
  - Customer satisfaction (NPS)
  - Revenue per customer
Choose winner for scaling
```

### Demo Format (Test 2 at a time)

```
A: Send PDF analysis (automated)
B: Schedule 15-min call (personal)
Both to 25 people, measure:
  - Conversion to paid upgrade
  - NPS score
  - Upsell rate
Winner wins
```

## Red Flags That Kill Startups (Avoid These)

### ❌ Flag 1: Ignoring Negative Feedback
- Customer says "price too high"
- You ignore it and scale
- Result: Low conversion rate, wasted ad spend

**✅ Fix:** If 3+ customers mention it, address it.

### ❌ Flag 2: Building Features Nobody Asks For
- You add "competitor benchmarking"
- Nobody cares
- Time wasted

**✅ Fix:** Only build what customers ask for.

### ❌ Flag 3: Not Following Up
- Customer buys, then never hear from you
- They forget about you
- No upsell, no referral

**✅ Fix:** Day 1, day 3, day 7, day 30 touchpoints.

### ❌ Flag 4: Wrong Target Customer
- Reaching out to cheap hosts
- Cheap hosts don't want to invest
- Low conversion rate

**✅ Fix:** Target high-price properties first (willing to invest).

### ❌ Flag 5: Unclear Value Proposition
- "Photo enhancement solution"
- Host doesn't understand why they should care
- Low conversion

**✅ Fix:** "Add photos of your hot tub, get +68% more bookings"

## Best Practices

### ✅ DO:

- **Listen more than you talk** (80% listening, 20% talking)
- **Ask "why?" 3 times** (Why didn't you like X? Why not? Why not?)
- **Take notes during call** (Show you care)
- **Follow up within 24 hours** (Act on feedback fast)
- **Share insights with team** (If you scale, your team needs context)
- **Celebrate small wins** ("We got first NPS 10!")

### ❌ DON'T:

- **Sell during interviews** (It's about learning, not closing)
- **Ask leading questions** ("You loved the photos, right?")
- **Interrupt** (Let them finish)
- **Get defensive** (If they criticize, listen)
- **Over-engineer based on 1 feedback** (Need pattern from 3+)
- **Forget to ask for referrals** (Every happy customer knows 5 more)
